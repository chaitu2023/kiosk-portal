Use of Cookies
Cookies are small bits of data cached or stored on your computer based on Internet activity. We may use cookies to monitor individual activity in aggregate to improve the Service and provide features that are tailored to your needs, including delivery of customized content and advertising. The information we gather may include IP address, user language, the operating system, browser type, the presence/absence of “flash” plug-ins, screen resolution, connection type, and information that identifies the cookie. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can usually change your browser settings to prevent this. If you disable cookies, your ability to use some features of the Service may be limited. We also use session cookies when you use the Service. Session cookies are active only during the period you are logged on to the Service, and are removed when you leave. They help us track the sections of the Service that you use to allow us to make improvements in keeping with observed user preferences. If you do not consent to our use of cookies, you should disable them or not access or use the Site or the Services. Otherwise, by not disabling the cookies or by accessing or using the Site or the Services you consent to our use of cookies.

Third parties may use cookies to make it easier for you to use our Services. The use of cookies by third parties, such as Facebook, Twitter, Google and Withings, are not covered by our privacy policy because we do not have access or control over these cookies. PLEASE CONSULT THE PRIVACY POLICIES OF SUCH THIRD PARTIES TO LEARN HOW THEY USE COOKIES.
