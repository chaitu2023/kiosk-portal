Description about Vitals
SPO2 SPO2 is a measurement of how much oxygen your blood is carrying as a percentage of the maximum it could carry. Body Fat Mass The body fat mass is the weight of fat in your body. The body fat percentage is the weight of the body fat mass in relation to the total body weight.
Temperature Temperature is the measure of hotness or coldness expressed in terms of any several scales, including Fahrenheit and Celsius. Intra Cellular Water Intracellular water is the water located inside your cells. It comprises 70% of the cytosol, which is a mix of water and other dissolved elements.
BMI Body Mass Index (BMI) is a person's weight in kilograms (or pounds) divided by the square of height in meters(or feet). Extra Cellular Water Extracellular water is the water located outside your cells. The water in your blood falls into this category. Roughly 1/3 of your fluid is attributed to ECW
Percent Body Fat The body fat percentage(BFP) of a human or other living being is the mass of fat divided by total body mass, multiplied by 100. Body Cell Mass Body Cell mass(BCM) is the metabolically active cell mass involved in O2 consumption, CO2 production and energy expenditure.
BP Systolic Blood pressure is measured using two numbers: The first number, called systolic blood pressure, measures the pressure in your arteries when your heart beats. Basal Metabolic Rate BMR is the rate at which the body uses energy while at rest to maintain vital functions such as breathing and keeping warm.
BP Diastolic Diastolic blood pressure, measures the pressure in your arteries when your heart rests between beats. Protein Content Protein content in any living organism is a result of the fine-tuned balance between two opposite rates:the rate of protein synthesis and the rate of protein degradation.
Pulse The rhythmic dilation of an artery that results from beating of the heart. Pulse is often measured by feeling the arteries of the wrist or neck. Mineral Content Minerals are those elements on the earth and in foods that our bodies need to develop and function normally.
Visceral Fat Viseral fat is fat that wraps around your abdominal organs deep inisde your body. You can't always feel it or see it. Bone Mineral Content Measures of bone mass include bone mineral content(i.e., the amount of mineral at a particular skeletal site, such as the femoral neck, lumbar spine, or total body).
waist to Hip Ratio The waist-to-hip ratio(WHR) is a quick measure of fat distribution that may help indicate a person's overall health. Waist to Height Ratio The waist-to-height ratio(WHtR), calculated by dividing the waist circumference(WC) by height.
Skeletal Muscle Mass Skeletal muscles compries 30 to 40% of your total body mass. They're the muscles that connect to your bones and allow you to perform a wide range of movements and functions.
