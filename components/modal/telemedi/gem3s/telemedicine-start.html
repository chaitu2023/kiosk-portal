Tele Consultation

Equitable access to healthcare services is a challenge for citizens of every country. This challenge is taking position in India due to limited resources and widespread settlements, especially in remote villages. In addition to issues around availability of healthcare organizations hurdles like affordability, remoteness, travel logistics, doctors' preference for cities, and so on are depriving appropriate and timely care. Such challenges fueled by healthcare seeking behavior are further enhancing severity of diseases and associated cost. Ideally most of such health issues can and should be managed locally and can be achieved through TeleMedicine.

TeleMedicine is a delivery system that uses telecommunication technology, information technology and Electronic Medical Record (EMR) and provides following major outcomes:

  • Minimize patient travel for healthcare services, especially during sickness

  • Decrease relocation of medical specialist

  • Enhanced quality of care

  • Continuity of care through EMR

  • Continuity of care through EMR

Concept

At the simplest for the main steps of this concept are:

  • Capturing clinical information at a remote site.

  • Transmitting such information to a specialist location.

  • Interpretation of such information and advice, along with video.

  • Storing entire interaction in database.

  • Making such information available to patients and authorized people.

Service Overview 

Every person looking at obtaining telemedicine services shall register with IHL . IHL will create uniquely identifiable EMR for each and every user. The demographic details and existing medicine is entered into their medical EMR to provide appropriate healthcare services whenever the user needs. This will enhance the quality of care. All clinical interactions are recorded and stored in the EMR and will be maintained until the agreed period of time. This EMR is accessible to all authorized persons (enrollee's explicit consent required) from anywhere in the world.

Introduction  

Telemedicine is the delivery of healthcare services when the healthcare provider and patient are not in the same physical location through the use of technology . electronically -transmitted information may be used for diagnosis, therapy ,follow-up and/ or patient education, and may include any of the following


  1. Patient medical records
  2. Interactive audio, video , and / or data communication.
  3. Put data from medical devices


  1. Purpose and benefits

    1. Tele-medicine enables patients living in rural and/ or underserved areas to get medical care by specialist without the inconvenience

    2. More efficient medical evaluation and management.


  2. Nature of Tele- medicine consultation :During the tele-medicine consultation:

    1. User has to decide on the doctor.

    2. After deciding the doctor , details of your health vitals will be Securely encrypted and shared with the doctor through the use of API (application program interface) call

    3. Users have to fill out the questionnaire also ( if required ).

    4. After that , there would be tele-medicine calls between patients and doctors.

    5. Your prescription will be shared through the mail and you can take a printout of the Same.


  3. Medical Information and Records

    1. All existing laws regarding your access to medical information and copies of your medical records apply to this tele-medicine consultation

    2. Please note, all telecommunications are recorded and stored (Patient data, Doctor data, doctor prescription are stored into Azure Data Base database except audio/ video record.)


  4. Possible risk

    1. Delays in medical evaluation and treatment could be due to the failure of internet connection . During this time , please wait for sometime to connect again with the Doctor. (If the connection does not come back, Page will redirect to the welcome page. If a patient is interested, call the doctor. Patients should log in their own account and recall to the doctor.)

    2. In rare cases, information transmitted may not be sufficient (e.g. poor resolution of images) to allow for appropriate medical decision making by the physician and consultant(s)
